Every prompt regressed.  Stop rule fired.

Why no win:

  1. Only 6 of 10 full-attn pairs fuse.  Q4_K_M has 6 attn_v as Q6_K and
     4 as Q4_K (per-layer policy).  Fusion guard requires Q6_K attn_v;
     other 4 layers fall through to unfused 2-call path.

     Verified: gemv_q4k call count delta = -768/run = exactly 6 calls/step
     × 128.  Expected -1280 if all 10 pairs fused.

  2. Per-call savings are dimensional noise.  6 fused launches/step
     replace 12 unfused → 6 launches saved × ~10 µs = 60 µs/step
     ceiling.  At 50 tok/s that's +0.15 tok/s — already below the +0.5%
     gate even before counting any kernel-side cost.

  3. Mixed-dtype kernel costs more per call than the two single-dtype
     kernels combined.  The inner loop runs:
       - 16 fmas for Q4_K dequant (16 elements)
       - 16 fmas + bit unpack for Q6_K dequant (16 elements)
     vs the unfused path where Q4_K and Q6_K each run their own optimized
     loop independently.  Per-lane register state ~doubles, IGC may
     spill, the doubled inner loop outpaces the launch overhead saved.

  All three converged to a small but consistent regression.

Lesson: mixed-dtype fusion is fundamentally different from same-dtype
fusion (ssm α+β, ffn gate+up, both clean wins at +1-2%).  In same-dtype:
fusion roughly halves kernel time per pair (shared SLM + shared compute
pipeline), so launch overhead saving is pure bonus.  In mixed-dtype:
kernel time stays at the SUM of both (no shared compute path), and the
launch-overhead saving alone isn't enough to clear the +0.5% gate when
only 60% of layers qualify for fusion.

Future fusion candidates should be filtered by:
  (a) same-dtype across all matched layers (avoid partial-fusion)
  (b) shape large enough that launch overhead ≥ +0.5% of decode time
      (small-N fusions like this one are dimensionally too small).
